Research is how we make sure that the products, services, and policies we create truly work for the people of New Jersey. It grounds our decisions in the lived experiences of real users — not assumptions or internal perspectives.

When we listen to people, we gain insight into what matters most to them, what challenges they face, and how government services can better meet their needs. This work helps us design better systems and builds trust by showing that residents' voices guide the process.

## Guiding principles

All research must be rooted in trauma-informed, ethical, and inclusive practices.

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Safety" icon="approve-check-circle">
    Participants should feel emotionally, physically, and psychologically safe throughout the process. We ask what they need to feel comfortable and take steps to prevent harm or re-traumatization.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Trust and transparency" icon="information">
    We explain our goals and methods in plain language, answer questions openly, and keep our commitments. Trust is built when expectations are clear and we follow through.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Choice" icon="approve-check-circle">
    Participants have control over how they take part in research. We offer options, respect their decisions, and recognize that people who have experienced trauma may value control even more.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Collaboration" icon="puzzle">
    We aim to share power in the process, working alongside participants and partners rather than treating them as subjects.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Empowerment and voice" icon="rocket">
    We validate participants' experiences, invite them to shape outcomes, and support them in making decisions and taking action.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Equity and inclusion" icon="star">
    We recognize cultural, historical, and gender disparities, and actively work to reduce bias in our teams, methods, and recruitment. Diverse voices strengthen our work.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

## Required training

<Aside type="caution" title="Complete before conducting any research">
Every team member and vendor must complete all three courses before conducting research with participants.
</Aside>

- [Trauma-Informed UI Research: Giving Care to the Participant and the Researcher](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xl2uVJir_80) — video, 47 minutes, by Zariah Cameron
- [Vulnerable Populations in Patient-Centered Outcomes Research and Clinical Care](https://phlearnlink.nwcphp.org/enrol/index.php?id=271) — online course, 1 hour
- [Understanding Types of Risk in Research](https://research.virginia.edu/irb-sbs/defining-risk) — University of Virginia guide, 10 minutes
