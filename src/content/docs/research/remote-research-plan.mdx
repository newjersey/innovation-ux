For small, lightweight research studies conducted remotely use Teams, Zoom, or UserInterviews. Best for usability testing, prototype validation, or short resident, business, or stakeholder interviews.

When to use:

- Testing prototypes or live websites with residents
- Interviewing residents or staff without needing in-person context
- Running short, focused usability or comprehension studies

Lean research:

- Remote sessions only. Use Teams or Zoom, scheduled through UserInterviews.
- Small sample size. Plan for 3–5 sessions — usually enough to surface recurring themes while staying efficient.
- Participant incentives. Distributed automatically through UserInterviews (researchers do not handle payments directly).
- Budget scope. Covers participant compensation only. No travel, lodging, or additional expenses.
- Simple research design. Keep the focus on clear, open-ended, plain-language questions. Avoid jargon and leading phrasing.
- Quick turnaround. Plan to synthesize findings within Dovetail immediately after sessions, so insights can be shared within days, not weeks.

## 2. Research Questions

Tips for writing strong research questions:

- Ask open-ended questions (e.g., “Tell me about the last time you…”).
- Avoid leading questions (don’t suggest the “right” answer).
- Avoid yes/no questions (they give little insight).
- Use plain, short language. Break apart “double-barreled” questions.
- Mirror back participant responses to check understanding.

Example: ❌ “Do you like this new homepage?” ✅ “What do you like or not like about this homepage?”

Recruitment platform: UserInterviews (handles screening, scheduling, incentives).

Recruitment Criteria

- Location: New Jersey resident (unless comparable local population is acceptable)
- Demographics: Representation across age, race/ethnicity, income levels
- Accessibility needs: Include participants with disabilities and varied tech literacy when relevant
- Language: Include Spanish-speaking participants if applicable (1-2 with support from bi-lingual Content Design Lead)

## 8. Research Risks

- Sensitive topics: Risk of participant distress if discussing benefits, disabilities, or personal hardships.
- Digital barriers: Some participants may have limited access to reliable internet or devices.
- Accessibility gaps: Need to ensure remote platforms are screen-reader friendly and allow captioning.
- Representation bias: Recruiting only online may exclude those with limited tech access.

## 9. Research Considerations

- Use trauma-informed practices when discussing sensitive topics (remind participants they can skip questions).
- Offer co-navigator option for participants with disabilities (e.g., caregiver present during session).
- Schedule sessions flexibly to accommodate participants’ availability. Connect your Outlook calendar to UserInterviews for hassle-free scheduling.
- If Spanish or other language support is required, plan for interpreters or bilingual moderators.
- Consider piloting the interview script with 1–2 people before full launch.
